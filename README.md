# AEM-Healthcare-Analytics

This repository contains analytics report for an interactive Power BI analytics project that provides insights into hospital operations, patient demographics, disease distribution, and treatment patterns. The dashboard enables analysis of patient admissions, condition severity, and healthcare utilization through KPIs, trends, and comparative visuals to support data-driven hospital management.

## Patient Overview
![alt text](<Visuals/Patient Overview.png>)

### Overall Insight

Patient demand is balanced across demographics, severity levels, and treatments, with mental health and routine care driving most hospital interactions.

### Key Metrics

- Total Patients: 25,000 (+4.2% MoM)
- Average Stay: 4 days
- Average Patient Age: 45
- Disease Types: 30
- Occupation Types: 8

### Patient Demographics

- Marital Status: Evenly distributed
  - Married: 34.12%
  - Single: 33.04%
  - Divorced: 32.84%

- Gender: Disease distribution is balanced across Male, Female, and Other, with no extreme dominance.

- Condition Severity
  - Mild: 33.39%
  - Moderate: 33.36%
  - Severe: 33.26%

 This indicates uniform case complexity and consistent care demand.

### Disease Insights

- Most common conditions: Anxiety, Arthritis, Food Poisoning, Allergy, Depression.
- Mental health (Anxiety) shows high prevalence across genders and marital statuses signalling attention. Slightly more pronounced in divorced patients.
- Disease distribution is consistent across Gender, Marital Status and Occupation segments, suggesting broad exposure rather than peculiar to a niche or risk groups.

### Occupation-Based Patterns

- Insignificantly higher disease incidence among:
  - Nurses
  - Engineers
  - Traders (being top 3)

Likely linked to high-contact or high-stress occupations (this margin is slight and not significant).

## Treatment & Visit Patterns

Treatment types evenly distributed:
 - Rehabilitation, Observation, Medication, Therapy, Surgery (~20% each)
- Top reasons for hospital visits:
 - Lab Tests (20.1%)
 - Follow-ups (20.08%)
 - Medication Review (19.95%)
 - Emergency & Checkups (~20% each)

### Summary

- Patient population is demographically balanced
- Disease severity is evenly spread, aiding predictable capacity planning
- Mental health conditions remain a major demand driver
- Non-invasive treatments dominate overall care
